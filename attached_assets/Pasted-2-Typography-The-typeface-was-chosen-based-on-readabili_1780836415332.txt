2. Typography
The typeface was chosen based on readability in Sarawak driving conditions including sun glare, rain and quick glances at driving speeds. Sans-serif and geometric display fonts were preferred to reduce stroke complexity and maintain clarity at a minimum reading distance of 60cm.
Element
Font
Usage
Rationale
Primary Label
Exo
Main dashboard labels, card titles 
Clean and modern typeface that supports readability of core interface information.
Secondary Label
Exo 2
Secondary information, supporting stats 
Softer rounded forms improve readability for smaller supporting content.
Button Label
Exo 2
Control labels, mode buttons 
Maintains visual consistency while remaining readable on small interactive elements.
Supporting Text
Exo 2
Captions, sublabels, descriptions 
Compact letterforms fit dense dashboard layouts without sacrificing clarity.
Mixed Text & Number Strings 
Exo 2
Range (200km), distance (200m), time, date, ETA, wind speed (km/h)
Used when numbers are paired with text units, providing better readability and consistency than a monospace font.
Pure Numerical Indicators
DM Mono
Battery %, temperature (°), PSI, humidity (%) 
Fixed-width digits maintain stable alignment as values update in real time.
Music timestamp 
DM Mono
Playback position and duration (0:00 / 3:21) 
Keeps time values aligned and prevents visual shifting during playback.
Dialer Keypad
DM Mono
Numeric keypad buttons  
Uniform character width creates consistent spacing across the keypad grid.
Dialer Number Display
DM Mono
Entered phone number
Fixed-width digits improve readability and maintain stable alignment as numbers are entered.
Call Timer
DM Mono
Ongoing call duration 
Stable digit spacing improves readability during continuous updates. 
Speed Display
Orbitron
Main speed meter 
Futuristic digital styling establishes a strong EV identity while making speed the most visually dominant information. Large geometric characters remain legible at a driving distance of 60–70 cm.
